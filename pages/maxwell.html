Maxwell Equations

The Maxwell Equations are 4 fundamental equations that describe the relation between the quantities of electromagnetism.

Name Integral Version Differential Version
Gauss's law: $${\iint}_{\partial \Omega} \vec E \cdot {\,\text{d}}A = \frac{1}{\epsilon_0} \iiint_{\Omega} \rho {\,\text{d}}V$$ $${\text{div}}\vec D = \varrho$$
Faraday's law of induction $$\oint_{\partial A} \vec E \cdot {\,\text{d}}\vec s = - \iint_{A} \frac{\partial \vec B}{\partial t} \cdot {\,\text{d}}\vec A$$ $${\text{rot}}\vec E + \frac{\partial \vec B}{\partial t} = 0$$
Gauss's law for magnetism $${\iint}_{\partial \Omega} \vec B \cdot {\,\text{d}}A = 0$$ $${\text{div}}\vec B = 0$$
Ampère's law $$\oint_{\partial A} \vec H \cdot {\,\text{d}}\vec s = \iint_{A} \vec j \cdot {\,\text{d}}\vec A + \iint_{A} \frac{\partial \vec D}{\partial t} \cdot {\,\text{d}}\vec A$$ $${\text{rot}}\vec H = \vec j + \frac{\partial \vec D}{\partial t}$$
